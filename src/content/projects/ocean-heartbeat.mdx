One night, one theme, zero 3D models on hand, and the drive to ship something that looks like a real project. Ocean Heartbeat is our first complete 3D web experience, built with Colin Demouge for Nuit de l'Info 2024 at Niort Tech.

## The project

The national challenge for this edition asked teams to **represent the parallels between the human body and the ocean**. We chose to turn it into an immersive experience rather than a standard informational website: an underwater 3D environment where the user explores organs (heart, brain, kidneys) embedded in an oceanic scene. Rocks, seaweed, starfish, and a lanternfish swimming around.

The idea was to **show those body/ocean connections in a visual and sensory way**, with ambient sound, animations, and the ability to switch between organs to discover the details of each parallel.

## The stack

We had just finished **Three.js Journey** and this was the perfect opportunity to put what we learned into practice on a real project under time pressure. React Three Fiber for the 3D scene, Drei for helpers, GSAP and Motion for UI animations, Zustand for global state management (sound, retro mode, selected organ).

For the models, no time to create them in a single night: everything came from **Poly Pizza**, convenient for licensing and especially to maintain a **consistent low-poly art style** across the entire scene. Positioning elements in the environment was done between **Blender** and **Three.js Editor**, a workflow we found quite efficient for fast iteration.

## What I took away

The real challenge of a hackathon is not coding features. It's **shipping something that feels finished**. We spent time on the details that matter: a clean loader, polished sound management, smooth transitions between views. And it paid off: we won the **"On veut du gros pixel"** challenge with our retro mode that pixelates the entire 3D scene through post-processing. An addition that took a few lines of code but delivered an immediate effect.

Most importantly, it confirmed that the skills we picked up from Three.js Journey were **directly applicable** to a real project, even under the pressure of a single night. Building a complete 3D environment from scratch in a few hours (scene, lighting, shaders, post-processing, interactions) gives you confidence for what comes next.
